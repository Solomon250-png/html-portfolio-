About COMAC (Commercial Aircraft Corporation of China)

Company Overview

COMAC, short for Commercial Aircraft Corporation of China, Ltd., is a state-owned aerospace manufacturer headquartered in Shanghai, China. Founded in May 2008, COMAC plays a central role in China’s ambitions to become self-reliant in large passenger aircraft manufacturing.

Key Aircraft Projects

ARJ21 Xiangfeng

  • Type: Regional jet (70–90 seats)
  • First flight: 2008
  • In service since: 2016
  • Operated mainly by: Chinese airlines

C919 Narrow-body Jet

  • Seats: 150–190
  • First flight: 2017
  • Entered service: May 2023
  • Main competitor: Boeing 737 MAX, Airbus A320neo

CR929 Wide-body Jet (In Development)

  • Joint project with Russia's UAC
  • Seats: 250–300
  • Status: Development delays
  • Target market: Compete with Boeing 787, Airbus A350

Strategic Significance

COMAC is a pillar of China’s long-term strategy to reduce dependence on Western aerospace technology. Supported under government initiatives like "Made in China 2025," COMAC aims to localize key aircraft components and boost domestic production capabilities.

Challenges

  • Certification outside China (e.g., FAA, EASA) is pending.
  • Still relies on Western suppliers for engines and avionics.
  • Global market entry requires building trust and infrastructure.
